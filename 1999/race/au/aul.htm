Pole position press conference

1. MH=Mika Hakkinen, McLaren-Mercedes, 1:30.462s
2. DC=David Coulthard, McLaren-Mercedes, 1:30.946s
3. MS=Michael Schumacher Ferrari, 1:31.781s

Q. Michael, in spite of having improved since this morning, you're still 1.3 seconds slower than Mika's McLaren. Does that represent the limit of the car at the moment?

MS: I wouldn't say it was the limit of the car. We have done a lot of work for reliability reasons over a Grand Prix distance and in fact we have struggled to get the performance out of the car. It does have potential, we know that, but we didn't use it this weekend, for various reasons which we experienced here for the first time. Going away from here, we obviously know what our next job will be.

Q. What is the biggest problem with the F399?

MS: There is no point in talking about it, to be honest.

Q. Mika, with you and David on the front row again, will there be another first corner agreement?

(MH and DC smile at each other)

MH (to DC): What do you think? (laughter)

DC: Only if I can win this time.

MH: We'll tell you tomorrow ... maybe.

Q. Mike, well done. Did you enjoy that?

DC: More than I did, to be sure ...

MH: I can enjoy it now, but at the time when qualifying was happening, the enjoyment was far away, I have to admit, because before my last lap David banged in a better time than I did. And when you have only one lap left to get in a time, it is very rare for it to succeed. You just have to get the right lap, and I did it. It worked, and I am very happy about it.

Q. Everyone's lap times seemed to vary during the session. Were the conditions changing out there a lot?

MH: No, they didn't change that much, but there were cars spinning off the circuit which brought dirt on to the surface. It got better and better all the way to the end, as normal.

Q. Are you surprised about the gap of almost half a second to David?

MH: That same question was asked last year, and I probably said no. I'll say no again. Although it is a comfortable gap, this is only qualifying. Tomorrow will count for points.

Q. Do you have the same feeling this year that you had 12 months ago?

MH: Yes and no. I obviously feel much more comfortable and confident with the whole situation, and I definitely feel I am a better driver now than I was at this time last year. I believe I am performing better than last year. I don't know how to continue talking about this ...

Q. David, did you think you had got pole position safely in hand?

DC: I did, but I had to sit and wait. I was a little surprised that it took Mika so long to go quicker in the session, because of the way we were running to do many laps rather than using too many sets of tyres. Then it was a question of getting a good, clean lap. And because I had to keep backing off in the traffic -- I think it was Johnny [Herbert] I was following for a few laps -- in the end I had given away too much time to cross the line and start [what should have been] my last lap. So I didn't complete all my qualifying laps and I would like to think that I was going to go quicker. Even when I did my 30.9 and saw that Mika was still on 31.0, I already had the feeling that we hadn't reached the maximum of the car. It was then I got the bad news that I had run out of time, so I had to come in anyway, to sit and wait. I am disappointed not to be on the pole. When I look at where we were running when we were popping the times in, I don't think the gap is a true reflection. Unlike last year, when the best times were set on the first flying lap with a fresh set of tyres, the more laps we were doing on the tyres, the quicker they were getting.

Q. Is this going to be a pattern for the year?

DC: I don't know, because this is an unusual circuit, being non-permanent. On the other tracks where there is more rubber laid down I don't expect qualifying times to be as close to last year's as they have been here.

Q. What reason would you suggest for so many drivers having gone off the circuit in the past two days?

DC: There is less grip available this year, the cars are more nervous to drive and tyre compounds are pretty hard now. When you just hit a bump and the car gets sideways, the momentum is enough to take the car off the circuit. We were told that the idea [behind grooved tyres] was to replicate driving in the rain, but the effect is completely different from that. It just means that at one moment you can feel quite stable, but you can go over the limit quite quickly.

Q. Is this what happened to you when you spun this afternoon?

DC: I think I did. I was surprised that I got sideways so quickly, it was probably because on that lap I was concentrating on keeping the brakes and tyres warm without going for a complete time. Maybe I just lost concentration slightly, put a tyre on the grass and spun it. It can be difficult having to change to a T-car that you haven't used up to that point, so I felt thankful that I was able to drive out of the gravel without damaging the tyres, and was able to continue running.

Q. What will be the important factors in the race?

DC: In any first race it is a combination of things. Reliability will be one. Strategy is always important, while tyre wear will be different from last year and we can't use the data we got then. We just need to hope. At the moment it looks like we have a car advantage and I would be surprised if that disappears when we go back to running with full fuel, because we were still quick [with high fuel] this morning and yesterday.

Q. Michael, you mentioned not being able to get the speed out of your car that you had hoped for. Are you paying the penalty for the loss of track time after you stalled on the track yesterday?

MS: I don't know whether that little amount of lost time would be enough to prevent us from getting our best performance.
